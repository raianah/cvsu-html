hristmas according to Wikipedia, is an annual festival
commemorating the birth of Jesus Christ that is observed
primarily on December 25 as a religious and cultural
celebration among billions of people around the world.

Christmas can be also a season of thanksgiving (for those
who don't have thanksgiving day) and family bondings,
creating happy memories with the family during winter.

As time goes by, with the booming of gadgets and
technology in general, Christmas seems to have lost
its true meaning. And all we have is Christmas without
your loved ones, especially during this pandemic.

Christmas in the early 2000s has been a great memories for
us, when we are kids. But as time goes by, the season of
Christmas seems to fade away. And with that here are the
5 reasons why Christmas will never be the same as 2012.

1) Responsibilities: As we grow up, our responsibility is
increasing. And probably as a functional working adult,
you rather have to take a rest than spending time with your
loved ones.

When we are kids (or toddlers), we don't think of electricity
/water/house bills, and all we have to do is to be play and
especially, be happy. Well as an adult, we don't do that here.

2) Being Busy: Having a lot of responsibility is there, and it
requires work. And with work, comes exhaustion. If we are
exhausted, we tend to rest all day, instead of going out and do
something. Trust me, you can't even play games and you might
want to spend your free time sleeping, or exercising.

3) Rise of Technology: Lastly, this factor affects people of all
ages. With technology lying around, people tend to just surf
on the internet all day instead of physically communicating
with someone somewhere.

Children are heavily affected by the technology, because
instead of playing with the people around them, they just play
with their gadgets all day. It does affect both physically and
mentally as they venture in the world of internet.

But no matter the reason, Christmas wasn't made yesterday.
The spirit of Christmas will live forever even the people will
change.
